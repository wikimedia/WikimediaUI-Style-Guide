Growing Wikipedia across Languages via Recommendations

We are developing systems to identify content gaps across Wikipedia languages and recommend contributions based on editors’ interests.

Project overview

Since the first English-language Wikipedia was launched, more than 150 language editions of Wikipedia have been developed. However, the comprehensiveness of each of these language editions varies, with almost 10% of the actively edited Wikipedia languages containing millions of articles and more than 60% containing 10,000 articles or fewer. Moreover, within all these wikis, there are many incomplete articles—known as article stubs—that need to be expanded to ensure they provide full encyclopedic coverage. Right now, for example, nearly 50% of all English-language articles are stubs.

To close these knowledge gaps between and within different Wikipedia language editions, the Wikimedia Foundation Research team began two sub-projects: one focusing on methods to identify, rank the importance of, and recommend missing articles for editors to create and one focusing on methods to identify and expose article stubs for further editing.
