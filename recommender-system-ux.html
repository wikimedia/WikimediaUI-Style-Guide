The User Experience of Recommender Systems

We are designing methods to understand the usefulness and usability of algorithmically generated reading and editing recommendations.

Project overview

The primary focus of this research is to develop methods for understanding the user experience of algorithmically generated recommendations.

Over the past few years, the Wikimedia Foundation has begun to build algorithmic models that automatically suggest articles for people to read, edit, translate, or create. The overall goal of these new features is to make reading and editing more engaging by helping users filter out “noise” (i.e., articles they are not interested in right now) and focus on the articles that are most relevant to their current interest, context, or activity.

Building these features and the models they are based on involves a series of important design decisions and tradeoffs. There are many ways to generate recommendations and just as many ways to present them to end users. The current project focuses on developing new methods to evaluate how well recommendation-based features support the anticipated use-cases of the intended audience? A second, equally important, focus of this project is to develop methods for detecting and characterizing potential biases in these algorithmic models that could prioritize some users' needs or interestes over others', reinforce existing biases in Wikimedia content, or lead to other unintended negative consequences.
