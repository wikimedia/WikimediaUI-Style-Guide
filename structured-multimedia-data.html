The Collaborative Production and Consumption of Structured Data

We are studying how collaboratively produced structured data impacts the curation and discoverability of multimedia content in free knowledge repositories.

Project overview

Wikimedia Commons is the world’s largest free-licensed media repository, with more than 40 million image, audio, and video files. Commons media are contributed by anyone and are curated by volunteers. The MediaWiki software platform that Commons is built on was designed to host text, not rich media. This creates challenges for everyone who uses Wikimedia Commons: media contributors, curators, and everyone who uses Commons media on Wikimedia projects and beyond. For example, the unstructured nature of Commons makes it difficult to capture important metadata for media files, upload them, and search for specific files.

The Wikimedia Foundation has launched a multi-year program to build a structured data layer into Commons based on the WikiBase platform. The research component of this project seeks to understand the impact of structured data integration on important Commons stakeholders, such as media contributors, curators, and consumers.

The primary focus of this research is to understand the current pain points and unmet needs of these stakeholders and the ways new structured data–driven functionality can address these needs. An important secondary focus is to understand core contributors’ and curators’ current workflows to minimize disruption to their work or undermine important ongoing processes during implementation of the structured data–driven functionality.
